# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Owner-operators of local service & hospitality businesses — restaurants, cafés & bubble tea, bars, bakeries, salons & spas, home services, retail, and small multi-location brands. They run the business *between customers*, not in long analysis sessions. They have no marketing team, no time, and no real read on their competition. They open a marketing tool for two minutes between a lunch rush and a delivery, and need it to hand them a decision, not homework.

## Product Purpose
Reputable is the growth engine that replaces a stack of five disconnected tools (a reviews monitor, a social scheduler, a "what should I post" guess, and no read on competitors) with one workspace. It watches reputation across Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, Uber Eats & Facebook; drafts on-brand replies; turns the week's viral trends into ready-to-shoot campaigns; and shows owners exactly where they stand against local rivals — with clear next steps they can act on the same day. Success = an owner replies on-brand in minutes (not hours), ships a trend-driven campaign without an agency, and watches their rating and response rate climb.

## Brand Personality
Self-assured, hospitable, alive. The confident hum of a place everyone's talking about. We speak the way a great maître d' carries a room — warm, in control, never flustered. Premium without being precious; this is software with the poise of a great restaurant. Emotional goals: confidence (you've got this), relief (finally, one place), and a little appetite (growth should feel exciting, not like a chore).

## Anti-references
- The generic SaaS purple/violet gradient on near-black — the first-order reflex for any "reviews/reputation" tool. (The source brief itself used it; we deliberately don't.)
- Hero-metric template (giant number, tiny label, gradient accent stat band).
- Identical icon-heading-text card grids repeated down the page.
- Tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section.
- Cold fintech "luxury" gold-on-black, and the warm-cream/editorial-serif reflex — here the surface is cool architectural stone, ink is graphite, and a muted camel is the only accent.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (thin italic serif on cream, ruled three-column metadata).

## Design Principles
1. **Restraint leads, camel earns its place.** Near-monochrome stone & graphite carries the brand (quiet luxury); a muted camel appears only where reputation is literally at stake (stars, a single emphasized word, one hairline).
2. **Show the product working, not a screenshot of it.** Imagery is live, crafted product UI — a review getting an AI reply typed in real time, a momentum chart, a competitor benchmark — not static mockups or stock gloss.
3. **Owner's words, owner's pace.** Copy is plain and confident. Every section ends with something the owner could act on today.
4. **Motion as hospitality.** Movement should feel poised and intentional — a room being set, not confetti. Earns attention, never demands it; always has a reduced-motion path.
5. **One dominant idea per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, art-directed sections over a uniform template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 on the velvet surface; large display ≥ 3:1. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation (smooth scroll, reveals, marquees, typing demo). Keyboard-navigable nav and CTAs with a visible brass focus ring. Motion never gates content visibility; everything renders without JS/animation.
